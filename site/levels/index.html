Level Guide

Every dancer progresses differently. Conexão’s levels are here to help you find the right starting point and move at a rhythm that suits your dance.

Each level combines the same five foundations, with a blend of more universitário and roots movement qualities.

  • TechniquePosture, balance, body mechanics
  • MusicalityRhythm, phrasing, listening
  • ConnectionComfort, clarity, dialogue
  • VocabularyFigures, transitions, movement range
  • Cultural awarenessContext, references, roots

New to forró?

Start with Level 1 unless a teacher suggests another level. You do not need a partner or previous dance experience.

How Levels Work

If you have danced forró before, compare the level descriptions below to see what level might suit you best. If you have questions, get in touch before registering.

If you're already following classes with Conexão and wondering about changing levels, have a think about:

  • Do I feel comfortable executing the figures that we learnt this trimester?
  • Was I able to attend the majority of classes?
  • Am I able to apply the different fundamentals that we learnt in? Fundamentals include things like rhythm, comfortable posture, a good hug, balance, tempo forte, breaks in the music, and the stages of leader-follower interaction.

Feedback Week, at the end of the trimester, is the best opportunity to check in with a teacher and decide together what level is best for you.

Typical Rhythm

As a guideline, we would expect most dancers to spend:

  • at least 1 trimester in Level 1
  • at least 2 trimesters in Level 2 and 3

This is a minimum, not a target.

There are lots of things which can influence how long you spend in each level. For example, flexi vs. full registration, illness or other absences, whether you review the class content or practise between classes, how regularly you are able to attend social dance events, and so on.

And there is no right or wrong rhythm: just the rhythm that works for you.

The aim is that every student is in a group where they will learn and progress at a rhythm that suits them.

Level 1

Good news! Everyone is welcome: no experience is needed for Level 1.

Level 1 is where we lay the foundations for your forró journey. We want to create good habits that will allow you to learn and grow as a dancer, and correct any “bad habits” which risk holding you back in the future.

Even if - or perhaps especially if! - you have experience of other partner dances, we recommend starting with Level 1. This is where you will learn how forró is both similar and different to other couple dances.

Roots 1

The roots offering is for dancers who want to go deeper into the roots side of forró. It focuses on a more specific movement quality and vocabulary than the regular level classes.

Earlier editions of this same offering were listed simply as Roots Course.

To register for Roots 1, leaders and followers need to master the roots base, square base, facão, pião, and caminhada.

If you are unsure whether this course fits you, ask before registering.

Roots 2

Roots 2 continues the same roots track at a more advanced stage.

To register for Roots 2, leaders and followers need to master the Roots 1 prerequisites, as well as trava, sacada, xique xique and paulista.

Short Courses

Short Courses are focused formats built around one topic over a shorter cycle than the regular trimester classes.

Dancing in Dialogue

Dancing in Dialogue explores different ways that leaders and followers can work together to play, dialogue and interact on the dancefloor.

If you already know the “stages” framework developed by Sheila Santos, this course corresponds to stages 3 and 4.

Footwork & Armwork

Footwork & Armwork focuses on how to use your arms and legs to create variations and express yourself.

In the same “stages” framework, this course corresponds to stage 2.
