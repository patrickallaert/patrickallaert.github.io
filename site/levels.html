Choose the right group

Level descriptions that help people register with confidence.

The point of this page is not gatekeeping. It is to make the weekly schedule easier to navigate and to help new and returning dancers join the right class.

Useful first step

If you are unsure, read the level descriptions here, then compare them with the weekly programme on the classes page before registering.

Go back to the weekly schedule

Level 1

For dancers starting Forró or rebuilding the basics carefully. Expect work on posture, frame, basic timing, simple rhythm, and confidence on the dance floor.

Roots 1

A first step into the roots track, for dancers who already feel stable with the foundations and want more depth in groove, connection, and musical understanding.

Level 2

For dancers who can already navigate the basics socially and want to improve lead-follow clarity, variations, and fluidity across a full class cycle.

Roots 2

For dancers with established fundamentals who want to work more seriously on rhythmic nuance, body organization, and vocabulary linked to roots aesthetics.

Level 3

For regular dancers with class experience, social floor awareness, and enough control to handle combinations, musical changes, and partner adaptability.

Level 4

For advanced dancers ready for dense material, precision work, and faster integration. This level assumes strong technical habits and independent practice.

Not sure where you fit?

Use the current registration flow and reach out if you need guidance. Matching people to the right room matters more than forcing everyone through the same path.
