Levels

Every person progresses differently. Technique, musicality, connection, vocabulary, and cultural awareness do not all grow at the same pace.

Conexão aims to develop well-rounded dancers, so each level combines these different aspects together with both universitário and roots movements.

How Levels Work

If you are new to Conexão, use the guidance below to orient yourself and then get in touch if you still have doubts.

If you are already following classes with Conexão and wondering about changing levels, think about the figures, attendance, and fundamentals from the current trimester. Feedback Week remains the best moment to check in with a teacher and decide together what makes sense next.

If You Are New to Conexão

  • Start with Level 1 if you are new to forró.
  • If you already dance forró elsewhere, read the level descriptions below first.
  • If you still hesitate, contact the team before registering.

If You Already Dance with Conexão

  • Do you feel comfortable executing the figures learnt this trimester?
  • Were you able to attend the majority of classes?
  • Can you apply the different fundamentals learnt in class: rhythm, posture, hug, balance, tempo forte, musical breaks, and interaction?

As a guideline, most dancers spend at least 1 trimester in Level 1 and at least 2 trimesters in Levels 2 and 3. This is a minimum guideline, not a target.

There is no right or wrong rhythm: the aim is simply that each student is in a group where they can keep learning and progressing.

Level 1

Good news: everyone is welcome. No previous experience is needed for Level 1.

Level 1 is where the foundations of your forró journey are built. The aim is to create good habits from the start and avoid patterns that might hold you back later.

Even if you have experience in other partner dances, starting with Level 1 is recommended. This is where you learn how forró is both similar to and different from other couple dances.

See current Level 1 classes

Level 2

Level 2 follows on from Level 1 and assumes that your basic foundations are already in place.

You are ready for Level 2 when you can use the fundamentals and figures from Level 1 in your dance with reasonable comfort.

The old site also pointed students to the Level 1 Vocabulary Playlist and PDF references. Those references can be brought back here later.

See current Level 2 classes

Level 3

Level 3 follows on from Level 2, where the aim is to develop body intelligence, familiarity with the fundamental figures, and confidence in the dance.

You are ready for Level 3 when you can comfortably use the fundamentals and figures from Level 2 in your dance.

The old site also pointed students to the Level 2 Vocabulary Playlist and PDF references. Those references can be brought back here later.

See current Level 3 classes

Level 4

Level 4 follows on from Level 3 and assumes a high level of comfort with the previous material.

You are ready for Level 4 when you can use the fundamentals and figures from Level 3 in a way that feels solid, comfortable, and musical.
