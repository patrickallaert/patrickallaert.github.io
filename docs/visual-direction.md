# Visual Direction

This note summarizes the Instagram references stored in `instagram-source/` and turns them into practical design guidance for the site rebuild.

## Source

- Reviewed local references in `instagram-source/1.webp` through `instagram-source/12.webp`
- Contact sheet generated for comparison: `instagram-source/_contact-sheet.jpg`

## Strong Patterns

- The dominant brand color is a vivid turquoise/teal.
- Warm yellow is the main accent color.
- White is used generously as a background or breathing space.
- Deep navy/blue appears in information cards and schedule blocks.
- Typography feels friendly, human, and community-oriented rather than corporate.
- Several posts combine a playful handwritten/display heading with a cleaner supporting sans-serif.
- Rounded cards and soft organic curves are recurrent motifs.
- Large abstract swooshes or ribbon-like white shapes are part of the current visual language.
- Photography is warm and social: embraces, dancing, couples, community moments.
- Many posts present information as posters or cards over a colored field.

## What To Keep

- The turquoise/yellow energy.
- The sense of warmth and welcome.
- The balance between practical information and community feeling.
- Organic curved background motifs derived from the logo and Instagram shapes.
- A layout language that can support posters/cards without turning the whole site into poster graphics.

## What To Improve For The Website

- Reduce dependence on text embedded in images.
- Use the Instagram visual language as atmosphere, not as the content model.
- Keep stronger hierarchy and spacing than the posts currently use.
- Increase contrast consistency where yellow on light backgrounds becomes weak.
- Make schedule information data-first and readable on mobile.
- Use photography more selectively so key actions remain obvious.

## Recommended Site Direction

- Base palette:
  - turquoise/teal as the main brand field color
  - warm yellow as the accent and CTA highlight
  - white or off-white as the main reading surface
  - deep blue/ink for text and structured information blocks
- Typography:
  - one expressive display face for selective headings only
  - one readable sans-serif for body, navigation, schedules, and forms
- Composition:
  - generous white/off-white reading panels over a more expressive colored background
  - occasional curved brand shapes in section backgrounds
  - strong card-based schedule blocks, but as real HTML content
- Tone:
  - clear, welcoming, lively, community-led
  - not corporate, not minimalist in a cold way

## Design Guardrails

- Do not rebuild the site as a stack of Instagram posters.
- Do not use decorative scripts for body text or dense information.
- Do not place essential schedule/location/price information only inside images.
- Keep mobile-first readability ahead of decorative density.
